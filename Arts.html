Arts

The arts comprise of visual art, dance, music, literary and performing, all of which women have contributed to throughout history. Barriers to success and acceptance in many of these spaces is still with us today, including stark statistic that within galleries in London, 78% represent more men than women, with just 5% having equal representation. Often these barriers inform their creative practices, at the Museum of Female Achievement we celebrate the creative expressions women throughout history have brought to the world.

Nina Simone (1993 - 2003, US)

Eunice Kathleen Waymon known professionally as Nina Simone, was an American singer, songwriter, musician, arranger, and civil rights activist. Her music spanned styles including classical, jazz, blues, folk, R&B, gospel and pop.

Rose Wylie (1934 - present, UK)

Rose Wylie is a painter, gaining great success later in her career. Wylie lives her life surrounded by her paintings, an all encompassing practice.

Sylvia Plath (1932 - 1963, US)

Sylvia Plath was an American poet, novelist, and short-story writer. She is credited with advancing the genre of confessional poetry and is best known for two of her published collections, The Colossus and Other Poems (1960) and Ariel (1965),

Frida Kahlo (1907 - 1954, Mexico)

Frida Kahlo was a Mexican painter known for her many portraits, self-portraits, and works inspired by the nature and artifacts of Mexico. Her style explores questions of identity, postcolonialism, gender, class, and race in Mexican society.

Virgina Woolf (1882 - 1941, UK)

Virginia Woolf was an English writer, considered one of the most important modernist 20th-century authors and a pioneer in the use of stream of consciousness as a narrative device.

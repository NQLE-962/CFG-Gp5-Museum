World Record Breaking Women

A collection dedicated to women who prove that mindset is everything. Visit us to see photography, medals, memorabelia and more!

Gertrude Ederle (1905-2003, US)

Numerous studies suggest that women outcompete men in various ultra-distance sports. In 1926, Gertrude Ederle swam the English Channel in 14 hours and 34 minutes, seven hours faster than what the first man achieved in 1875.

Junko Tabei (1939-2016, Japan)

Founder of the Ladies Climbing Club, the first of its kind in Japan, Junko Tabei was the first woman to reach the peak of Mount Everest (16th May 1956). She then became the first female to ascend all Seven Summits (the highest peak on every continent) and, in her lifetime, reached the highest peak in 56 nations around the globe.

Libby Riddles (1956, US)

On March 20th, 1985, Libby Riddles became the first woman to win the “last great race on Earth” — the 1,100-mile Iditarod Trail Sled Dog Race. Dog mushing for 18 days in -50° weather, she won by continuing on in a blizzard while all the other competitors waited out the storm.

Mira Rai (1989, Nepal)

Growing up in a remote village in the Bhojpur mountains, Mira Rai's formal education ended at twelve. With limited opportunities available, she joined the Maoist rebel army training in trail running, karate, and shooting. Rai participated in her first ultra-marathon by mistake, a simple training run in Kathmandu turned out to be a 31-mile race. She was the only person to compete without water, food, or specialized gear. She won the female division. Since then, Rai has achieved a new course record at the 50-mile Marathon du Mont-Blanc and won second place in the Skyrunning World Championships.
